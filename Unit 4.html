Unit 4

    ★  Safety:

  • ➤    Safety is the state of being free from harm or danger. It is an important aspect of any work environment, and it is the responsibility of employers to ensure the safety of their employees.

    • ★  Responsibilities and Rights:

  • ➤    Employees have certain responsibilities and rights in the workplace, such as the right to a safe and healthy work environment, the right to fair wages and benefits, and the responsibility to follow workplace policies and procedures.

    • ★  Safety and Risk:

  • ➤    Safety and risk are closely related. Risk refers to the possibility of harm occurring, while safety is the state of being protected from harm. In the workplace, it is important to assess risks and take measures to ensure safety.

    • ★   Assessment of Safety and Risk:

  • ➤    The assessment of safety and risk involves identifying potential hazards, evaluating the likelihood and severity of harm, and taking measures to reduce or eliminate risks.

    • ★  Risk Benefit Analysis and Reducing Risk:

  • ➤    Risk benefit analysis is a process of evaluating the risks and benefits of a particular action or decision. It is important to reduce risks as much as possible while also considering the benefits of the action.

    • ★  Collective Bargaining:

  • ➤    Collective bargaining is a process in which employees negotiate with employers as a group to determine working conditions, wages, benefits, and other aspects of their employment.

    • ★  Professional Rights:

  • ➤    Professional rights refer to the rights of individuals who work in a particular profession. These may include the right to practice their profession, the right to fair compensation, and the right to a safe and healthy work environment.

    • ★  Employee Rights in Professional Ethics:

  • ➤    Employee rights in professional ethics include the right to be treated with respect and dignity, the right to confidentiality, and the right to report unethical or illegal behavior without fear of retaliation. Employers have a responsibility to uphold ethical standards and ensure that employees are aware of their rights.
